EXOHUNTER

As diploma thesis for our school a colleague and I developed a tool to find extrasolar planets. The idea is to find traces of planetary existence in the brightness of distant stars. In theory, a stars brightness should decrease if a large object, say a planet ten times the size of earth, transits in between the observer and the light source itself. This periodic dampening of brightness can be detected by looking at patterns in light curves. The brightness data over certain time periods is provided by NASA and is publicly available.

The tool utilizes a pattern recognition neural network, which is trained to find unusual behaviour in star luminosity. Once the planets are determined, further parameters can be calculated. By looking at the interval of occurrence, strength of dimming, and duration of the transit, properties like radius, mass, distance to star, etc. are easily determined. We can even tell if a planet is likely to be habitable or hostile towards life as we know it. The idea is to use a peer network that shares computing power to process the enormous amounts of data provided by NASA and ultimately find new planets that haven't been discovered before.

DEEP SKY STACKER

The goal for this project was to automate the process of stacking photos of the night sky. As a result of small camera error a single image can be too noisy. To reduce noise, multiple shots are taken in a series before they are then stacked on top of each other. However, due to earths rotation, the sky shifts a few degrees between those shots, which makes the task of stacking photos difficult. Deep Sky Stacker finds the same stars in a set of images and uses them to align the samples, so the median/mean values can be determined. The tool is written in C# and utilizes multi-threading to cope with the high performance demand.

3D ADVENTURES

In 2015 I discovered 3D programming and have been practising it ever since as a hobby. Most of my 3D projects are in correlation with procedural generation. I am obsessed with the idea to generate landscapes, objects, trees, even entire planets by just using mathematics. I achieved this goal by using Unreal Engine 4(C++) and mainly Unity(C#) as a 3D engine. I have studied ways to make procedural generation as realistic looking as possible, while also maintaining performance using multi-threading and GPU utilization.
